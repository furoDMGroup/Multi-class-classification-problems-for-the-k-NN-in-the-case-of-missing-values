Multiclass classification problems for the k-NN in the case of missing values

This website shows results of the following paper: U. Bentkowska, J. G. Bazan, M. Mrukowicz, L. Zaręba, P. Molenda, "Multi-class classification problems for the k-NN algorithm in the case of missing values", FUZZ-IEEE 2020 submitted. In the paper 8 datasets from the well known UCI repository was taken to evaluate quality of our algorithm, called MF. It is a multiclass extension of the binary classifier, called F, proposed in the paper: U. Bentkowska, J. G. Bazan, W. Rząsa, and L. Zaręba, "Application of interval-valued aggregation to optimization problem of k-NN classifiers for missing values case", Inform. Sci., vol. 486, pp. 434–449, 2019.

The research was made in the Institute of Computer Science, College of Natural Sciences, University of Rzeszów.

The aim of the experiments was to compare numerical modelling with interval-valued modelling. In order to achieve this result we implemented also the binary classifier, called M, which is based on the WEKA library approach. The multi-class version of this algorithm is called MM.
The presented results show AUROC (Area under ROC) values calculated for both MF and MM classifiers. The experiments were made using stratified k-fold cross-validation. The AUC is an average value of a series of k-folds results. The standard deviation was also computed. In both algoritms MF and MM a parameter k of the nearest neighbours is involved. Moreover, in Algorithm MF two parameters, i.e. r and aggregation are involved. The obtained results for the given multiclass algorithms proved the superiority of interval modelling over numerical modelling.
